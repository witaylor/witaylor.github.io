    • TravelHack

    TravelHack is a Expedia Group (EG) hackathon, organised and run by interns, for students. The main target group for participants was students who had applied to EG for an internship. There were two core ideas behind the running of TravelHack;

    1. Give people the opportunity to become more familiar with Expedia Group and their offices in London.
    2. Allow recruiters and managers to see potential candidates in an almost real-world work environment, and not just an interview.

    Alongside helping with organisational and planning tasks, I also designed and created two websites for TravelHack. One was used before the event for promotional purposes, and was designed to give applicants more information about who Expedia Group are and what TravelHack is. The other was designed to be a one-stop-shop for all information during the event. It had a list of expample traveller problems/scenarios to solve, a list of the volunteers helping out and more features.

    The first, promotional website was made using HTML and CSS, with the only JavaScript being for Google Analytics. I was the sole developer of this site, but collaborated with another developer on the design.

    The second, information website was initially created using React.js (with JavaScript) by me and another developer. I have since re-written the site from the ground up, using React.js with TypeScript. This was done in an effort to make the site easily customisable and reuseable for the next iteration of TravelHack.
